1787 Prestonian Lectures Q. What is the ancient clothing of a Mason?
A. White gloves and a white leather apron.
Q. What do the first denote?
A. Purity.
Q. What does the second denote?
A. Innocence.
Q. How are they both considered?
A. Ensigns of the Order of Masonry: more ancient and honorable than the ensigns of any other Order; and always to be worn by Masons as the badge of innocence and the bond of friendship.
1790 Harodim I invest you with this Apron, as a Badge of Innocence, and Bond of Friendship, which is more ancient than the Golden Fleece, or Roman Eagle, more honorable than the Star, Garter, or any other Order under the sun
1802 Browne To invest me with the white apron; it being the distinguishing badge of a mason, which, the senior warden, by the right worshipful master,s command, was pleased to inform me, was more ancient then {= than} the Roman Eagle or Golden Fleece and more honourable than the Star and Garter or any other order under the sun, unless it was a higher degree in masonry, and gave it me in the strongest recommendation, ever to wear it as the badge of innocence and the bond of friendship; and he likewise further told me, that if I never disgraced that badge, it would never disgrace me.
1802 Finch The distinguished badge of masons, which I was Invested with, by order of the Master by the Senior Warden, telling me it was more antient and honorable than the Star or Garter, the Thistle or Rose, or any order whatever under the Sun; it being the badge of innocence and bond of friendship and giving at the same time a suitable charge upon the occasion.
1802 Webb That peculiar ensign of masonry the lamb-skin, or white leather apron, which is an emblem of innocence and the badge of a mason; more ancient than the golden fleece, or Roman eagle; more honourable than the star and garter, or any other order which might be conferred upon him at the time of his initiation, or at any time thereafter, by king, prince, potentates, or any other person except it be a mason; and which every one ought to wear with equal pleasure to himself, and honor to the fraternity.
